Biography

Hello! I'm a future web developer, and I'm happy to be part of the FLS course.

Since I was a child, I had friends who encouraged me to pursue programming. However, from the age of 6 until I turned 20, I played hockey professionally.
In short, programming wasn't really on my radar during that time.

Many of my peers spent a lot of time on computers, while I mostly watched movies. I avoided downloading games because I was worried about getting viruses,
which created a somewhat unusual problem for me. Thankfully, I managed to overcome this only recently.

For the past three years, I've been working with computers, and I noticed that when I encountered a problem, I tended to avoid solving it. I'm referring to more complex issues, not everyday situations.
Basic tasks didn't intimidate me, but I never dared to reinstall Windows, for instance. Most of the time, I sought help from someone who knew how to solve the problem, and I rarely attempted to figure it out on my own.
Even when I tried searching online, I often gave up after just a few minutes.

Recognizing this tendency, I made a conscious effort to change it. It wasn't easy, but my work demanded it. I started tackling smaller problems and gradually gained more confidence.

Today, I have gathered enough experience to dive into web development. I'm determined to put in the effort to learn and become the best student in the course.
